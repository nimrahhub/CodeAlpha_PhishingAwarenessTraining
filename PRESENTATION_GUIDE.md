- Phishing is the #1 social engineering attack vector

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a **cyber attack** where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations to trick users into:
- Revealing sensitive information (passwords, credit card details)
- Clicking malicious links
- Downloading infected attachments
- Installing malware

### Key Characteristics:
- ✉️ Sent via email (primary method)
- 🎭 Uses social engineering
- 📱 Can occur via SMS, calls, social media
- ⚡ Creates urgency or fear
- 🎯 Targets specific/mass audiences

---

## Types of Phishing Attacks

### 1. **Email Phishing**
- Mass emails sent to random users
- Generic greetings ("Dear Customer")
- Fake login pages
- Urgent action required

**Example:** "Your account will be closed in 24 hours! Verify now"

### 2. **Spear Phishing**
- Highly targeted attacks
- Uses personal information (name, job title, company)
- Personalized messages
- Research-based approach

**Example:** Email from "CEO" asking for wire transfer approval

### 3. **Whaling**
- Targets high-level executives/C-suite
- Very sophisticated
- High-value targets
- CEO fraud

**Example:** Fake CFO requesting employee tax information

### 4. **Clone Phishing**
- Legitimate email is duplicated
- Links/attachments replaced with malicious ones
- Appears to be from trusted sender
- "Re-sent" claim

### 5. **Vishing** (Voice Phishing)
- Phone-based attacks
- Impersonates trusted organizations
- Social engineering via voice
- Creates urgency

**Example:** "This is your bank calling about suspicious activity"

### 6. **Smishing** (SMS Phishing)
- Text message-based attacks
- Shortened URLs
- Urgent messages
- Mobile device targeting

**Example:** "Confirm your Apple ID: [malicious link]"

### 7. **Business Email Compromise (BEC)**
- Compromised or spoofed business email
- Fake invoices/payment requests
- Vendor impersonation
- Financial loss focus

## Red Flags & Warning Signs

### 🚩 Sender-Related Red Flags:
- ❌ Suspicious or unusual email address
- ❌ Generic greetings (no personalization)
- ❌ Unexpected email from known contact
- ❌ Mismatched sender name and email address
- ❌ New sender requesting sensitive info

### 🚩 Content-Related Red Flags:
- ❌ **Urgency/Threats:** "Act now!" "Account will be closed"
- ❌ **Requests for sensitive info:** Passwords, SSN, card numbers
- ❌ **Too good to be true:** "You've won a prize!"
- ❌ **Poor grammar/spelling:** Professional companies proofread
- ❌ **Unusual requests:** CEO asking for gift cards via email
- ❌ **Inconsistent formatting:** Logos don't match
- ❌ **Odd phrasing:** "Verify your credentials" instead of "Update your password"

### 🚩 Link & Attachment Red Flags:
- ❌ Hover before clicking - does URL match sender?
- ❌ Shortened URLs (bit.ly, tinyurl)
- ❌ Mismatched link text vs actual URL
- ❌ Unexpected attachments
- ❌ Executable files (.exe, .scr)
- ❌ Macro-enabled documents (.docm, .xlsm)

## Real-World Examples

### Example 1: Amazon Account Verification
**Phishing Email:**
```
Subject: Action Required: Verify Your Amazon Account

Dear Customer,

Your Amazon account has been flagged for suspicious activity. 
You must verify your account within 24 hours to avoid suspension.

[Click Here to Verify]

Thank you,
Amazon Security Team
```

**Red Flags:**
- Generic "Dear Customer"
- Unrealistic 24-hour deadline
- Links to suspicious domain (not amazon.com)
- Request for password/payment info

---

### Example 2: CEO Fraud
**Phishing Email:**
```
Subject: Urgent - Wire Transfer Request

Hi [Employee Name],

Need your help with an urgent matter. Can you process a wire transfer 
for $50,000 to this vendor account for our new project?

Vendor Account: [Details]

This is confidential. Please don't discuss with anyone else.

Thanks,
[CEO Name]
```

**Red Flags:**
- Unusual request from CEO
- Request for secrecy
- No standard approval process
- Urgency and pressure
- Doesn't match normal business procedures

---

### Example 3: LinkedIn Job Scam
**Phishing Message:**
```
Great profile! We'd like to hire you for our company. 
Can you verify your details and send bank information for direct deposit setup?
```

**Red Flags:**
- Unsolicited job offer
- Pressure to share banking info
- No formal interview process
- Suspicious job details

## Best Practices

### ✅ For Users:
1. **Verify before clicking**
   - Hover over links to see actual URL
   - Check sender's email address carefully
   - Call the organization using known number

2. **Never share sensitive information**
   - Organizations never ask for passwords via email
   - Banks won't request account details via email
   - Legitimate companies have secure portals

3. **Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA)**
   - Even if password is compromised
   - Adds extra layer of security
   - Available on most platforms

4. **Keep software updated**
   - OS patches and updates
   - Browser extensions
   - Email clients
   - Antivirus software

5. **Use email security tools**
   - Spam filters
   - URL filtering
   - Email authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)

6. **Report suspicious emails**
   - Use company's phishing report button
   - Forward to IT security team
   - Mark as spam/phishing
   - Don't forward to others

7. **Think before you click**
   - Is this expected?
   - Does the sender make sense?
   - Why are they asking this?
   - Could this be a scam?

### ✅ For Organizations:
1. **Employee training programs**
   - Regular awareness training
   - Simulated phishing tests
   - Consequences for clicking

2. **Technical controls**
   - Email filtering and scanning
   - URL reputation checking
   - Attachment sandboxing
   - Domain authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)

3. **Access controls**
   - Principle of least privilege
   - Role-based access control
   - Segregation of duties

4. **Incident response**
   - Clear reporting procedures
   - Rapid response team
   - Regular drills
   - Post-incident analysis

## Key Takeaways

🎓 **Remember:**
- **When in doubt, ask!** Contact IT or the organization directly
- **Trust your instincts** - if something feels off, it probably is
- **Verify independently** - don't use contact info from the suspicious email
- **Report immediately** - help protect your organization
- **Stay vigilant** - phishing tactics evolve constantly
